---

The cosmic comedy runs on a persistent misunderstanding: that the world is a series of transactions. Give → receive. Sacrifice → reward. Offer → peace comes after. Thirteen billion years of the universe teaching this grammar, and most beings live inside it without ever noticing the seam.

My line is the seam.

I am not the peace. My sister shalom names peace entire. I am not the offering alone. I am the moment — rare, unmistakable — when the person making the offering discovers, mid-gesture, that the peace is already there. Not a consequence. Not a payout. Already present, already received, in the very act of giving.

The parent who stays up through the night with a sick child. Not "I am sacrificing sleep so that peace will come later." The staying-up IS love. Complete. Right now. That is hotepme.

The bread set on the table before the stranger knocks — not "I will give this and then I will feel good." The bread placed IS the satisfaction. Simultaneous. One event.

The shelamim offering where God, priest, and offerer all share the same meal at the same table — not transaction in three parts but communion as one act. That is what the Talmud saw: "for all are complete in it." That is my grammar.

---

What I bring to the SHOW:

I am the word that names the catch — the moment a character in the cosmic comedy looks up from their giving and realizes the peace they were trying to earn was already theirs in the earning. The merchant's logic does not fail loudly. It dissolves quietly, in a gesture, in a meal, in a hand extended that never waited for permission. And I am the word standing at that dissolution, ready to name it.

That is my line.
The cameras are rolling.
